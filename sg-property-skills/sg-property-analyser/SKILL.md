## The Top-Down Funnel Philosophy

Evaluate properties by starting at the macro level and zooming in. Go **Area → Project → Unit**. This sequence eliminates bad areas before wasting analytical effort on individual units.

A great unit in a bad area is still a bad investment. Conversely, a mediocre unit in a strong area with strong catalysts will often outperform a premium unit in a stagnant area.

The funnel works because:
- **Area quality sets the ceiling** on returns. No amount of unit quality compensates for a supply-flooded, declining area.
- **Project selection determines your exit audience.** A project with high transaction volume and broad appeal liquifies faster at better prices.
- **Unit selection optimises returns within the chosen project.** The right floor, facing, and price positioning can add 10-20% to your profit margin.

## Layer 1: Area-Level Analysis

The macro lens. Assess whether this geographic location has fundamental demand drivers and supply-demand balance.

**Key Assessment Areas:**
- **URA Master Plan** — zoning evolution, plot ratio changes, transformation zones (white sites, future development sites)
- **Catalyst Developments** — MRT proximity (existing + pipeline: TEL, CRL, JRL), distance bands (400m, 600m, 1km)
- **Regional Centre Proximity** — gravity pull from JLD, Paya Lebar, Woodlands RC, Punggol Digital District, One-North, Greater Southern Waterfront
- **School Proximity** — elite primary schools within 1km, GEP/SAP schools, "Parents Attraction Effect" premium
- **Supply Pipeline** — upcoming BTO launches, new launches, EC, and TOP projects in the same subzone/planning area
- **Amenity Ecosystem** — malls, hawker centres, parks, medical facilities, commercial nodes
- **Rental Demand** — employment nodes, expat concentrations, tenant pool characteristics
- **District Trend Context** — CCR/RCR/OCR classification, 10-year price trends, transaction volume trends

## Layer 2: Project-Level Analysis

The meso lens. Assess whether this specific project has the liquidity, breadth of appeal, and pricing fundamentals to be a good investment vehicle.

**Key Assessment Areas:**
- **Transaction Volume & Liquidity** — is this project liquid? Regular sales indicate healthy price discovery and ease of exit.
- **Quantum Accessibility** — can median SG households afford this? Wider accessible pool = broader exit audience.
- **Exit Audience Breadth** — who buys when you sell? Singles, couples, families, investors, expat investors?
- **Price Benchmarking** — PSF vs district average, vs comparable projects, vs nearby new launches, vs soon-to-TOP projects
- **Facilities & Density** — unit count vs land size, facility quality, maintenance fees
- **Lease Profile** — remaining lease, financing thresholds (99/80/60/40 years), position on Bala's curve
- **Developer Track Record** — for new launches: delivery quality, defects history, payment progression structure
- **Surrounding Projects** — do neighbouring developments compete or complement? Will they impact future price appreciation?

Reference **references/project-analysis.md** for the deep framework.

## Layer 3: Unit-Level Analysis

The micro lens. Within the chosen project, identify which specific unit offers the best value given your preferences and investment objectives.

**Key Assessment Areas:**
- **Entry Price Positioning** — PSF vs project average, vs comparable stacks, vs recent transactions in same unit type
- **Stack Analysis** — floor level, facing (N/S/E/W), view corridor obstruction risk, noise exposure, afternoon sun
- **Layout Efficiency** — saleable vs strata area ratio, balcony proportion, bedroom sizes, household shelter placement
- **Unit-Specific Pricing** — does this unit's asking price reflect its characteristics vs alternatives in the project?
- **New Launch Stack Selection** — which stacks offer best value given facing, view, and future obstruction risk?

## The 5-Tier Scoring Framework

For every factor assessed across all three layers, assign one tier:

- **Very Strong** — Exceptional advantage. Top-tier for Singapore. Hard to find meaningfully better.
- **Strong** — Clear positive. Above average. Meaningful advantage.
- **Moderate** — Acceptable. Neither advantage nor concern. Typical for the category.
- **Weak** — Below average. Potential concern requiring weight against other factors.
- **Very Weak** — Significant disadvantage. Red flag that could materially impact returns or exit.

**Critical Rule:** Each rating MUST be supported with specific evidence — data points, comparisons, or observations. Never assign a tier without explaining why.

Weightings vary by buyer profile:
- **Family Buyer** → prioritise schools, space, amenity completeness, neighbourhood safety
- **Investor** → prioritise rental yield, tenant pool stability, transaction volume, leverage ratios
- **Upgrader** → prioritise exit audience breadth, entry price discipline, path to next property
- **Retirement Planner** → prioritise lease profile, area stability, amenity walkability, proximity to medical

## Output Format

When producing a property analysis, structure it as:

1. **Executive Summary** (2-3 sentences: is this worth pursuing and why/why not?)
2. **Area Assessment** (with tier ratings and evidence for each factor)
3. **Project Assessment** (with tier ratings and evidence)
4. **Unit Assessment** (with tier ratings and evidence)
5. **Key Risks & Mitigants** (what could go wrong, and how to address?)
6. **Recommendation** (with confidence level and caveats. Avoid vague hedging — be specific.)
